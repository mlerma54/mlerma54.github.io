Class Logistics

  • Textbooks

    Infinite Series
    C.H. Edwards, Jr. & David E. Penney: Calculus with Analytic Geometry, 5th edition, Prentice Hall, chap. 11. Note that the edition is different from the one used in the Winter quarter.
    Linear Algebra
    Leonard Evens: Sequences & Series, Linear Algebra (Notes), Northwestern University. Available at Quartet (on Clark Street, West of Sherman). There is a new edition for this quarter, but its contents are the same as in the Winter quarter, only the covers are different.

  • Discussions

    The discussion sessions will be held on Thursdays under the supervision of the TAs.

    Students whose last name initial is A-L go to Tech M166 (Kreisle).
    Students whose last name initial is M-Z go to Tech L158 (Vaynberg).

  • Homework

    Homework will be assigned daily but not collected. Several assignments will consists of Maple Worksheets, and these will be collected on the discussion session and graded by the TA.

  • Maple Worksheets

    Look at http://www.math.nwu.edu/maple/ for information about Maple Worksheets.
    Look at http://www.math.nwu.edu/maple/b17/ for the worksheets for B17.
    Troubleshooting: http://www.math.nwu.edu/maple/help.html.

  • Quizzes

    There will be short quizzes based on the homework from previous lectures on the Thursday discussion sessions.

  • Exams

    There will be two one-hour Midterm Exams and one two-hour Final Exam.

    • First Midterm: Thursday, April 15, 1999, 12:00-1:00pm. Infinite Series.
    • Second Midterm: Thursday, May 13, 1999. Linear Algebra, chapters I and II.
    • Final: Wednesday, June 9, 1999, 12:00-2:00pm. It will include questions from the whole syllabus.

    Calculators are not allowed in the exams. No make-up exams will be given. In the event of an extreme and well documented absence (such as hospitalization) the final may be weighted to count for the missing exam. In the case of a missed exam, contact the instructor as soon as possible.

  • Grading

    The course will be graded as follows:

    • Quizzes: 20%
    • Maple Worksheets: 10%
    • Midterms: 40% (20% each)
    • Final: 30%

    The lowest quiz score will be dropped in calculating the quizzes grade. There will be no make-up quizzes. If you miss a quiz it will be dropped as your worst quiz grade. The lowest maple worksheet grade will also be dropped.

  • Add/Drop Policy

    If you want to change sections, or add/drop the course, please do so at the Math Department Office in Lunt.
